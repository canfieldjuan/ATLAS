# Report Catalog & Strategy

## **1. Types of High-Value Reports**
Prioritize reports that answer critical questions for your buyers (e.g., procurement teams, vendor managers, or competitive intelligence analysts). Focus on **actionability** and **exclusivity**—data they can't easily get elsewhere.

### **A. Churn Risk Alerts (Hot Leads)**
**Purpose:** Identify vendors with high churn risk, so buyers can poach dissatisfied customers or avoid risky partnerships.
**Format:** Short, urgent, and action-oriented.

#### **Example Report: "Churn Risk Alert: [Vendor Name]"**
| Section               | Content                                                                 | Value to Buyer                          |
|-----------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Vendor Overview**   | Name, industry, customer base, recent news.                            | Context for decision-making.            |
| **Churn Signals**     | Aggregated sentiment trends, spike in complaints, topic clusters (e.g., "support delays," "pricing hikes"). | Identifies why customers may leave.     |
| **Risk Level**        | Low/Medium/High + confidence score.                                     | Prioritizes outreach efforts.           |
| **Affected Segments** | Company sizes, industries, or regions most impacted.                   | Helps target the right prospects.       |
| **Competitor Gaps**   | Areas where competitors are outperforming (e.g., "Vendor X's onboarding is 30% slower than Y"). | Highlights opportunities to win deals.  |
| **Actionable Insights**| "Target companies mentioning 'poor support'—they're 2x more likely to churn." | Directly drives sales outreach.         |
| **Recommended Plays** | Scripts for sales teams (e.g., "Position your 24/7 support as a differentiator"). | Reduces friction for buyers.            |

**Delivery:**
- **Dashboard alert** (e.g., "New high-risk vendor detected").
- **PDF/email report** for sharing with sales teams.
- **API access** for CRM integration (e.g., "Push high-risk leads to Salesforce").

---

### **B. Competitive Benchmarking (Warm Leads)**
**Purpose:** Help buyers compare vendors and spot weaknesses in their competitors' offerings.
**Format:** Visual, comparative, and data-rich.

#### **Example Report: "[Industry] Vendor Benchmark: Q2 2026"**
| Section               | Content                                                                 | Value to Buyer                          |
|-----------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Vendor Scores**     | Sentiment, NPS, feature satisfaction (e.g., "Vendor A: 7.2/10, Vendor B: 8.5/10"). | Objective comparison tool.              |
| **Trend Analysis**    | How scores changed over time (e.g., "Vendor A's support scores dropped 15% post-acquisition"). | Spots declining competitors.            |
| **Strengths/Weaknesses** | Top 3 strengths/weaknesses per vendor (e.g., "Vendor B excels in API reliability but lags in customer training"). | Informs positioning and messaging.      |
| **Customer Quotes**   | Anonymous but segmented reviews (e.g., "'Their reporting tool is clunky'—Mid-Market SaaS Company"). | Adds credibility and nuance.            |
| **Switching Triggers**| Common reasons customers leave (e.g., "40% of churn mentions 'lack of integrations'"). | Guides sales messaging.                 |

**Delivery:**
- **Interactive dashboard** (filter by industry, company size).
- **Static PDF** for executive sharing.
- **Blog teaser** (e.g., "Which [Industry] Vendor Has the Highest Churn? [Free Excerpt]").

---

### **C. Vendor Deep Dives (Warm Leads)**
**Purpose:** Provide exhaustive intelligence for strategic decisions (e.g., partnerships, acquisitions).
**Format:** Long-form, data-dense, with expert analysis.

#### **Example Report: "Vendor X: Strengths, Risks, and Customer Sentiment"**
| Section               | Content                                                                 | Value to Buyer                          |
|-----------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Customer Profile**  | Who uses them (industries, company sizes, geographies).                | Helps identify target accounts.         |
| **Sentiment Trends**  | Monthly sentiment scores + drivers (e.g., "Sentiment dropped after May 2025 pricing change"). | Predicts future churn.                  |
| **Feature Analysis**  | What customers love/hate (e.g., "Their analytics dashboard is praised, but mobile app is criticized"). | Informs product roadmaps.               |
| **Churn Predictors**  | Patterns linked to churn (e.g., "Companies using <3 features churn 50% more"). | Flags at-risk customers.                |
| **Competitor Overlap**| Companies using Vendor X + a competitor (e.g., "20% of Vendor X's customers also use Vendor Y"). | Identifies cross-sell opportunities.    |
| **Expert Take**       | Your analysis of why trends matter (e.g., "Vendor X's churn spike aligns with their layoffs in support"). | Adds unique perspective.                |

**Delivery:**
- **Gated PDF** (lead magnet for blog).
- **Webinar** (live breakdown + Q&A).
- **Dashboard module** (drill-down into specific vendors).

---

### **D. "Battle Cards" for Sales Teams (Hot Leads)**
**Purpose:** Arm sales teams with real-time intel to win deals.
**Format:** Concise, bullet-pointed, and CRM-friendly.

#### **Example Battle Card: "How to Win Against Vendor X"**
| Section               | Content                                                                 | Value to Buyer                          |
|-----------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Vendor Weaknesses** | Top 3 complaints (e.g., "Slow response times," "Hidden fees").         | Talking points for sales calls.         |
| **Customer Pain Points** | Direct quotes from reviews (e.g., "'Their contract lock-in is predatory'"). | Builds empathy and urgency.             |
| **Your Differentiators** | How your product solves these issues (e.g., "Our transparent pricing vs. their hidden fees"). | Closes deals faster.                    |
| **Objection Handlers**| "If they say 'Vendor X is cheaper,' respond with [script]."             | Boosts win rates.                       |

**Delivery:**
- **CRM integration** (e.g., HubSpot/Salesforce).
- **Slack bot** (e.g., "@churnsignals battlecard Vendor X").
- **Printable one-pager** for field sales.

---

### **E. Industry-Specific Reports (Warm Leads)**
**Purpose:** Attract niche buyers (e.g., "Churn Trends in FinTech, 2026").
**Format:** Thematic, with actionable takeaways.

#### **Example Report: "Why FinTech Startups Are Ditching Vendor A"**
| Section               | Content                                                                 | Value to Buyer                          |
|-----------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Industry Trends**   | Macro trends (e.g., "FinTech churn spiked 25% in 2026 due to compliance changes"). | Contextualizes vendor risks.            |
| **Vendor Rankings**   | Who's winning/losing in FinTech.                                        | Helps buyers shortlist vendors.         |
| **Case Studies**      | Anonymous examples (e.g., "A Series B FinTech saved $50K/year by switching to Vendor B"). | Social proof.                           |
| **Risk Mitigation**   | "Avoid vendors with <90% uptime if you're in payments processing."     | Reduces buyer's risk.                   |

**Delivery:**
- **Gated content** (email capture).
- **LinkedIn carousel** (teaser stats).
- **Partner co-branding** (e.g., with a FinTech association).
